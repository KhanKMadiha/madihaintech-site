How I got here

I'm the sole EMEA support engineer at Stack Overflow, owning every P1/P2 incident across 100+ enterprise accounts. None of the systems I built to do that job were asked of me. I spotted the gaps, built the solutions, and documented them so they scaled beyond me.

That instinct didn't come from a traditional route into tech. During the pandemic I started teaching myself to code out of pure curiosity. That curiosity led me into customer-facing roles, then into product support, and eventually into the kind of work I do now: debugging APIs, reproducing issues in internal environments, and making sure engineering gets evidence, not just reports.

What drives me

🤝

I genuinely enjoy helping people

It's the reason I got into support in the first place and the reason I'm still here. There's something genuinely satisfying about turning a frustrated customer into a confident one, and I never get tired of it.

🔎

I investigate, not escalate

My 95% issue reproduction rate did not happen by accident. I use Datadog, Elasticsearch, Postman, and browser DevTools to trace problems to their root before engineering ever gets involved. They get answers, not questions.

🏗

I build infrastructure, not just fixes

I authored 50+ knowledge base articles that reduced repeat tickets by 20%. I built Datadog dashboards that cut P1/P2 resolution time by 25%. I designed onboarding workflows that reduced ramp-up time by 30%. All of this was self-initiated.

🤖

I am genuinely interested in AI

Not as a buzzword on a CV. I completed IBM AI Fundamentals, hold certifications in prompt engineering, and built and deployed a real Claude-powered chatbot on this site, secured via a Cloudflare Worker proxy. I use Claude as a daily work tool.

I communicate across complexity

Fluent in English, Urdu, and Hindi, with intermediate Punjabi. I have owned enterprise relationships across EMEA, de-escalated P1 incidents on live calls, and translated technical root cause into language that works for every room.
